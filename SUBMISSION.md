The project "An Implementation of OHDSI OSS on InterSystems IRIS" represents a strategic shift toward making the technical setup of OHDSI tooling "automatic". After reflecting on the 2025 OHDSI Symposium, I realized that effort directed at the "left side"—infrastructure and implementation—often misses the ultimate point of the OHDSI mission, which is to produce valid scientific insights. I identified a critical need to bridge the gap between complex technical deployment and the generation of observational evidence on the "right side" of the CDM, essentially getting to the point without duplicating efforts.

By accelerating the implementation process and making it “automatic”, this reference architecture helps researchers bypass the "learning curve" of infrastructure to finally "get to the point" of observational evidence generation, The resulting solution utilizes InterSystems IRIS Community as the primary database for the OMOP Common Data Model (CDM) and its standardized vocabularies, serving as the stable midpoint for this journey and celebrating its recent inclusion in the OHDSI stack.

Scheduled for presentation at the 2026 OHDSI Symposium, this initiative is designed as a high-performance, non-commercial pathway for the community. The architecture onboards the comprehensive OHDSI open-source suite—including ATLAS, WebAPI, HADES, DQD, and Achilles—as containerized services within an IrisCluster. Drawing inspiration from the Broadsea distribution, the solution is built for both enterprise deployment and local evaluation using Kubernetes workload patterns.

A core innovation of this implementation is Agentic ETL, which leverages the Google Agentic Development Kit (ADK) to streamline the transition from FHIR resources to the CDM. Rather than replacing established OHDSI tools, the agentic AI consumes the scan reports and mapping "hints" generated by WhiteRabbit, Rabbit-in-a-Hat, and Usagi. By using these outputs as foundational logic, the AI agent automates the generation and execution of transformation code, significantly reducing the manual effort typically required to populate an OMOP instance.

The project emphasizes transparency and personal aspiration of contributing to the OHDSI community. I will be adopting the OHDSI-compliance framework, providing documentation templates for a Record of Processing Activities (ROPA), an Information Security Concept, and an Operational Concept specifically for the IRIS stack. All configuration scripts and container definitions will be shared openly from a single source. Furthermore, I assume full accountability for the "heavy lifting" involved in ensuring IRIS interoperability, aiming to empower researchers and organizations without increasing the support burden on existing OHDSI maintainers.
